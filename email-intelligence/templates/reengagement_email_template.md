# Re-engagement Email Template — Ghosted Lead Recovery

Use these patterns when a prospect opened a proposal but went silent. The framework is built on three principles validated against real cycle-time data: short, value-focused, and decision-driving.

## Core principle

In most B2B pipelines, deals that don't close within the first week rarely close at all. Re-engagement emails should either revive the conversation immediately or surface a clean "no" so you can stop investing effort. **They should never feel like nurturing.**

---

## Pattern 1 — High-intent re-engagement (multiple opens, recent)

Use when the prospect opened the proposal more than once, especially within a short window. This signals real consideration.

```
Subject: Still relevant?

Hi [Name],

I noticed you reviewed the proposal — twice. That usually means
something caught your attention.

If there's an open question, a concern, or a piece that didn't quite
fit — I'm here. Five minutes on a call and we'll know if this is the
right move.

[One sentence on differentiator: speed / specialty / track record]

What do you say?

[Your name]
```

---

## Pattern 2 — Single-open re-engagement (segment-specific)

Use when there's a single open and no follow-up signal. Lead with the segment-specific value, end with a direct binary question.

```
Subject: [Service area] — at the standard you expect

Hi [Name],

You reviewed our proposal in [month]. We haven't heard back since.

If you have a project that needs [specific deliverable in their
vertical] — we deliver it at the level [their role / industry]
expects: [3 specific quality markers].

One question: is the need still there?

[Your name]
```

---

## Pattern 3 — Long-elapsed re-engagement (3+ months out)

Acknowledge the gap. Don't pretend it didn't happen. Pivot fast to value, then ask cleanly.

```
Subject: [Service] — [Brand]

Hi [Name],

You saw our proposal back in [month]. I know it's been a while.

If there's still a need for [specific service category] — [one short
sentence on what makes you the right partner].

Bottom line: if the need is still there, let me know.

[Your name]
```

---

## Pattern 4 — Holiday / seasonal context re-engagement

Use when the proposal was opened during a known low-engagement window (end of year, summer, major holidays). Reframe the gap as circumstantial.

```
Subject: [Service] — picking up in [new period]?

Hi [Name],

Our proposal was opened at the end of [period] — a busy time. Things
probably fell through the cracks.

If you're planning [relevant project type] in [new period], now is a
good time to lock in a partnership.

[One sentence on why now matters / why you fit]

[Your name]
```

---

## Variables to customize

| Variable | What to fill in |
|---|---|
| `[Name]` | Recipient first name (or formal title for legal/finance) |
| `[Service area]` | Your specific offering (e.g., "legal translation", "technical localization") |
| `[Specific deliverable]` | What they actually need (contracts, marketing copy, regulatory docs) |
| `[Quality markers]` | 3 concrete differentiators — accuracy, deadline, domain expertise |
| `[Differentiator]` | Your single sharpest competitive edge for this segment |
| `[Period]` | Specific time reference (Q4, December, summer, fiscal year-end) |

---

## What to avoid

- **Long preambles.** "I hope this email finds you well" — delete.
- **Apologetic framing.** "Sorry to bother you again" — signals weakness.
- **Open-ended questions.** "Let me know your thoughts" — drives nothing. Use binary asks.
- **Multiple CTAs.** One ask per email. Pick the one decision you actually need.
- **Vague value claims.** "We deliver high quality" — show, don't claim.

---

## When to send

- **Single open, no response after 24h** — Pattern 2 (high priority window)
- **Multiple opens, no response after 24h** — Pattern 1 (highest intent signal)
- **No response after 7 days** — Final attempt, then move to long-tail
- **30+ days elapsed** — Pattern 3 with a hard binary close
- **Quarterly re-engagement campaign** — Pattern 3 or Pattern 4 depending on context

After 14 days with no response, treat as lost. Re-engagement after that point is a quarterly campaign, not active sales follow-up.
